## One-Sentence Overview

A single-player quest level with both interior and exterior spaces, independently designed and developed in the Starfield Creation Kit, using spatial layout, combat design, and environmental storytelling to create a two-ending mission built around a moral choice.

## Game Overview

Starfield is an open-world science-fiction RPG developed by Bethesda Game Studios. Built with the official Creation Kit, this project adds an original, independently playable side quest to the game.

## Level Overview

Decaran: Become Human is a Starfield side-quest level. The player is hired to investigate an abnormal incident at the automated Decaran III factory and gradually uncovers the truth behind the robots' awakening. As the story unfolds, the player must ultimately choose between restoring factory production and helping the robots gain their freedom. The two choices lead to dramatically different endings.

## My Work

1. Narrative Design
• Designed the complete side-quest narrative, mission objectives, and two-ending structure.
• Wrote NPC dialogue, quest text, and environmental storytelling content.
• Used a moral dilemma to encourage players to reflect on the relationship between robots and humans.

2. Level Design
• Designed the factory's overall spatial layout, player routes, and exploration flow.
• Built a level rhythm that alternates among combat, exploration, and puzzle-solving.
• Used thematic zoning to strengthen spatial navigation and environmental readability.

3. Gameplay Design
• Configured enemies and combat encounters.
• Designed core gameplay elements including EMP weapons, laser obstacles, and terminal puzzles.
• Used Papyrus Script to implement quest logic and event triggers.

4. Playtesting and Iteration
• Continuously refined flow, combat difficulty, navigation, and environmental storytelling through multiple rounds of playtesting.
• Fixed scripting issues and completed the final build.

## Project Retrospective

What Went Well
• The overall visual presentation was strong.
• I attempted and implemented several new design ideas.
• The level's vertical spatial structure was well designed.
• I continuously refined the level flow based on milestone reviews and playtest feedback.

Even Better If
• Communication with stakeholders, including instructors and producers, could have been stronger.
• The laser-platforming gameplay did not perform as well as intended and should have received further polish if time allowed.
• The narrative content was still insufficient.
• Combat difficulty was too low and lacked challenge.
• Parts of the flow still needed improvement.
• The laser mechanic was not used to its full potential.
• The exterior area was relatively underdeveloped.
• The overall level quality still fell short of my expectations.

What I Learned
• Proactively communicate with stakeholders and respond to feedback promptly.
• Plan development time more effectively.
• Gain a holistic command of the engine and tools as early as possible in a project.
• Improve my ability to adapt to complex and unfamiliar development tools.
• Commercial game engines and internal company tools are imperfect, and designers must learn to work with their limitations.
• Complete a full level project according to a professional game-development pipeline.
• Time and energy management are crucial to a project's success.
• Design and refine level flow.
• Find a balance between gameplay and believable architectural logic.
• Build game narratives that are more complete, clearly structured, and supportive of gameplay.
